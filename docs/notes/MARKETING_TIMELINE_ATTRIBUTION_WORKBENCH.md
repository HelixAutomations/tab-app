# Marketing Timeline Attribution Workbench

> Purpose: active project brief for turning the Marketing tab into a financial-year-to-date attribution surface.
>
> Verified: 2026-06-15 against the current Marketing tab direction. Re-verify after the first timeline component lands.

## 1. Core Direction

Starting with the 2026 financial year, Marketing should stop feeling like a report picker and start feeling like an evidence workbench. The default lens is financial-year-to-date from 01 Apr 2026 onward. The page should load into that frame, show the feeds cooking, then present channel activity and conversion evidence under one roof.

The broad message for Alex: we are building a single view that proves what marketing activity is happening, where it is happening, what source it creates, and where it converts. Charts and ROI come after the evidence spine is trustworthy.

## 2. Product Shape

- Remove the manual entry choice between current week, current month, last month, and 90 days.
- Replace it with a processing cue that shows the financial-year-to-date pull and feed settlement breakdown.
- Keep the summary banner joined to the channel workbench so time, channel, and evidence feel governed by the same surface.
- Treat SEO and PPC as the first governed channel tabs. Later add email, LinkedIn posts, content pushes, and campaign activity.
- Add a timeline-style component below the banner/channel ribbon. It should show team efforts across channels before the user reaches evidence quality and KPI strips.
- Keep source confidence, Teams-visible intake, and pitch follow-up as validation cards below the timeline, not as the page's primary story.

## 3. Timeline Spine

Initial lanes:

| Lane | Evidence |
|---|---|
| SEO | GA4 sessions, organic enquiries, content milestones, conversion path. |
| PPC | Ads spend, clicks, paid enquiries, conversion and cost evidence. |
| Email | Campaign sends, replies, intake attribution once source data is reliable. |
| LinkedIn | Posts, content pushes, engagement, eventual enquiry impact. |
| Gaps | Unknown source, unclassified records, missing attribution links, cleanup queue. |

## 4. Phases

1. FYTD lock: auto-open the marketing workspace on current financial year and make the loading state feel intentional.
2. Timeline spine: add the cross-channel activity timeline below the summary banner and above the evidence/KPI strips.
3. Channel expansion: bring email, LinkedIn, content, and campaign notes into the same workbench model.
4. Proof and ROI: surface three-month comparison, attribution gaps, conversion evidence, and ROI by channel once data is clean.

## 5. Acceptance

- Marketing entry no longer asks the user to choose a reporting window.
- FYTD processing cue shows which feeds are loading, settled, or unavailable.
- The channel workbench and timeline share one visible governor.
- Evidence quality and intake KPI strips sit below the timeline as validation.
- Three-month versus three-month comparison is introduced only after the FYTD baseline is stable.

## 6. Meeting Note

Use this as the compact direction-of-travel artefact for the Alex conversation: FYTD baseline first, timeline proof second, ROI later once attribution is dependable.
